So, yeah. For those of you who don't know me or are not in contact with me for sometime, I have to tell you that, unfortunately, I am a smoker. I'm not proud of being a smoker and I wish I have never lit up that first cigarette, about 15 years ago.

Throughout this period, I have accomplished to quit smoking twice. One time for about 2.5 years, and the other one for about 1.5 years. And I did it by going full cold turkey, as I am planning on doing once again.

The reasons why I got back smoking were because I had some episodes in my life that for me were very hard to cope with, and I was weak enough to succumb to the slavery of the cigarettes. I don't know how some people do, but knowing myself as I do, I could never be that person who can only smoke socially. That was what got me back into this trap. If I think that I can only smoke while hanging out with friends, I'm mistaken. That unique cigarette just for fun, turns out to be another unique cigarette the next day, and another 2 the day after, and when you realize, you are already buying a pack of cigarettes once again.

Last week I managed to stay 3.5 days cigarette free. I was already beginning to see some results, my skin was clearing, my mouth was becomming to taste food the way it should be tasted, again. My nose was able to recover a little of its sense of smell. It was all going greate, until the COVID-19 situation came upon us. The company I work with told us all to work from home, and that was just enough to make me buy another pack of cigarretes.

You see, the battle against this addiction is mental. You have to be strong and you have to be strongest whenever an odd situation presents itself.

Finally, I am writing these lines so, maybe, someone in a similar condition might want to read and relate. The ultimate goal would be for this someone to find it inspiring, and try to quit too ( if that someone happens to be you, please contact me somehow and let me know :-) ).

I will quit smoking tonight, and I'll keep you updated over the next weeks with my progress and thoughts.

Let's be strong, let's own our lives, let's say no to tobacco.
