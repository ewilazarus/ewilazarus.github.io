I’m a brazilian programmer born on the year of 1988 in the city of Rio de Janeiro. Although I have lived in many places and even in different countries, I’ve spent the most time living in Brazil.

At the age of 20, I discovered my passion about computer programming when I was already at the university. The first programming assignment I had was to write a program in C. It was a very simple assignment, where I had to read two integers from the user input, and output the sum of those integers. The moment that thing ran in front of my eyes, it was like a bomb that blew my mind. From that moment on, I knew that this programming thing had unlimited possibilities and that I wanted to learn to do it well, so I could do it for the rest of my life.

I ended up specializing in back-end development, although I also like to do some tweaking in the devops area and will do front end development if necessary. I’m a big Microsoft fan and I’m really excited about the .NET (Core) ecosystem as a whole. Recently I started learning F# to complement my C# skills and I never looked back.

In my professional life I had the opportunity to work alongside very talented people and, from them, learn a lot. I worked my whole life for the technology market, but right now, for the first time in my career, I’m into the financial market, working on some awesome projects for enabling automation of bank related stuff and the “fintechyalization” of the biggest investment bank in Brazil.

I am really keen to stuff like high availability, distributed systems and will always attend to conferences and meetings whenever I can.

Aside from the tech related description, my hobbies are weight lifting every day in the morning, playing videogames and watching movies.
